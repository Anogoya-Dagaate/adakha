My Life, Some Story

Kerygma in Anamnesis

By Anogoya Dagaate

I was born on the first of September 1981 it seems ominous to me because This is all that remains for me, a man. Men are cursed with the search for order and meaning. like the woman in the novel who can forgive her husbands infidelity but simply cannot accept her place exchanged for nothing! Nothing? He left me because I was worse than nothing? Another woman is dignifying - if she's attractive, sadistically more so. 'The Pig!' He'll leave after my own beauty had waned from bringing to life his seed Such impudence!

One of my earliest memories growing up was the sense of beauty - I was a sensitive child. A mystic from birth I cried freely, at music, at suffering - an almost existential sorrow. I was keenly aware that man suffered. Not that this suffering was inevitable - for we had TV's! There was a place where food came out of ovens and fridges held leftovers. I somehow knew though - that such a place was not mine, worse yet - that mine was envied! A nascent spirituality overlaid all my experience hence; that a God must obtain and the same understand and distribute as seemeth fit

Portrait of the philosopher . . . as a young man! Such a curious thing, that birds should yet preserve feet as if they numbered among the wingless!

If you commit to the difficult path - without recourse to shortcuts An easier path may yet be revealed; if you reject the hard-road even it assumes impossibility which in truth is but long and painful

It behoves the man to commit to such as he must. Its a sure path to damnation this search for shortcuts.

The challenge faced in learning is one of humility. I just spent a couple of hours, at least one changing a "\" to a "/". Now I could have saved that entire hour if I had not assumed such a foolish error below my 'pay grade'.

May God grant us always the humility to remember how weak and unstable we are; how helpless and hapless without His aid

Szegedy-Mazsak represents for me the great chaos

Of our human existence. That a people who have so rejected Their God should without repentance suffer in his name beyond reconciliation. The verdict may be that light came into the world but men preferred darkness - certainly from malice but surely also from ignorance and stupidity. The terror of leaving ones cave with its familiar smells and satisfactions like Graham Greens unforgetable description of masturbating prisoners.

What is this we call the Jew? Who is he and what crime hash Done, what sin adjudged guilty of? The thing of which I'm bothered is this - that men should entertain sin at all - here's a thing at once cancerous and of insurmountable vitality - to bring such forth? Why one might as well wish for ones own death - which indeed one does!

The miracle is that anything should be able to undo the ravages of sin - it is an immolation. The organism succumbing to its own death in hopes that in resurrection the agent of torment having perished should no longer rise with this new life.

"For this has the Son of Man come into the world;

TO DESTROY THE WORKS OF THE DEVIL!

Behold, I make all things new!"
